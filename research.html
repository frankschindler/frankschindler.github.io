Research Interests

A large part of my work so far has focussed on topological states, an emerging field in condensed matter physics that promises to have wide-ranging applications in microelectronics as well as providing a possible avenue towards a quantum computer.

My further research interests include collaborating with experimentalists and developing numerical techniques for strongly-correlated systems. Another focus are quantum many-body scar states and other obstructions to thermalization, which may allow us to realize exotic ground-state physics at finite temperatures. Recently, I have also studied excited states in twisted bilayer graphene, a material that seems to realize some properties of high-temperature superconductors in a much more tractable context.

The common theme of my current work is the generalization of concepts from topological band theory to systems beyond symmetry indicators, free fermions, linear oscillators, Hermitian Hamiltonians, and ground states.

Light Reading

There are nice pedagogical introductions to topological phases of matter at Quanta Magazine and also here. For more technical reading, see the lecture notes at the site of the Les Houches summer school on Topological aspects of condensed matter physics.

Another good starting point -- particularly for higher-order topological phases -- are our Lecture notes on topological crystalline insulators, my tutorial on a Dirac equation perspective on higher-order topological insulators, as well as the introductory section of my PhD thesis.

You can furthermore find a list of press releases and news & views articles about our work here:

Novel Insulators with Conducting Edges, about our theoretical work on higher-order topological insulators. For a general overview that also covers the contributions from other groups, see Topological Insulators Turn a Corner (by Siddharth A. Parameswaran and Yuan Wan).

Topology reaches higher spheres and A little bit of everything (by Christian R. Ast), about our discovery of Bismuth as a higher-order topological insulator. See also our collaborators' press release at EurekAlert.

Topology on a breadboard (by Ling Lu), about our realization of higher-order topology in an analog electric circuit experiment.

Chirality meets topology (by Chandra Shekhar), about our prediction of a variety of novel Weyl semimetals.
