1. The barometric height formula
We want to find how air density \(\rho\) is related to height from ground \(h\) in the atmosphere. At temperature \(T\) and in the thermodynamic limit, we expect \(\rho\) to be given by the Boltzmann distribution $$\rho(h) \propto \exp\left\{- \frac{E(h)}{k_B T}\right\},$$ where \(E(h)\) is the energy of particles at height \(h\) and \(k_B\) is Boltzmann's constant. When we plug in the standard potential energy formula \(E(h) = m g h\) that is valid close to earth's surface, with \(m\) the mass of air particles and \(g\) the gravitational acceleration, we retrieve the barometric height formula.
